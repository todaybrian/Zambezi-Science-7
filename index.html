About Zambezi River:

Zambezi River is one of the most beautiful river in the world. It is the forth longest river in Africa, being 2200 miles (3540 kilometers) long, and it the longest river flowing into the Indian Ocean. It flows through 6 countries: Zambia, Angola, Namibia, Botswana, Zimbabwe, and Mozambique. It holds hundreds and hundreds of unique species, and it is the pathway to Victoria's Fall.

Abiotic Factors:

Air

  • Provides C02 to plants, helps plants grow
  • Gives animals oxygen to breath and releases C02 (which helps plants grown and restarts the cycle)
  • Spreads seeds around (wind), increasing the population of plants
The plant around Zambezi River is getting it's C02 to help it grow, which makes Oxygen

Water

  • Helps animals around the river, like elephants live around the water, providing them with a fresh drink and helping their body function (like Temperature Regulation)
  • Helps fish and other living items inside the water, which provides animals, like the eagle with food.
  • Helps plants, since they need water, to grow, which helps other animals.
The Hippo is swimming in the Zambezi River

Soil

  • Gives nutrients to plants, which helps animals have food.
  • Holds worms and other life in soil
  • Holds and stabilizes water.
  • Soil is the foundation of all living plants around the Zambezi River.
Dirt around the Zambezi River

Sunlight

  • Sunlight is nutrition to the plants.
  • Light helps plants live, grow and reproduce.
  • Helps animals see.
  • Provides heat.
  • Animals get Vitamin D.
Sun in Zambezi River

Producers, Consumers and Decomposers

Producers

  • Plants (Small shrubs and ferns, much more), Flowers, Trees (Ebony, much more), Algae, Litchen around zambezi river get their energy through photosynthesis (abiotic element)
The trees around Zambezi River are taking in light, which helps them grow

Consumers

    There are a lot of consumers around and inside the zambezi river
  • Elephants - Use their trunks to grab grass (biotic element)
  • Fish - May eat worms, smaller fish and plant life (biotic elements)
  • African Fish Eagle - Uses their claws to grasp fish (biotic elements)
  • Hippopotamus - Graze on land (biotic element)
  • Crocodiles - Hunt unsuspecting animals and birds where they come to drink and feed (biotic element)
  • Giraffes - Use long necks to eat leaves, seeds, fruits, buds and branches (plants) (biotic elements)
An African Fish Eagle is grabbing fish from the Zambezi River

Decomposer

  • Worms around the river eat organic matter like grass and scraps of other plant life (breaking down organic matter) and changes it to a fertilizer to help create nutrients in soil, which helps plants grow (biotic), which helps animals get their energy
  • Fungi that are in zambezi river have been feeding on dead fish and other living animals have (breaking down organic matter) been sadly spreading diseases to other animals. (Source: https://www.voanews.com/a/a-13-2009-07-21-voa22-68701257/409248.html Title:Fungal Disease Decimating Fish in Zambezi River)
  • Dung beetles decompose and break down manure from other animals, like elephants, hippopotamus and other animals that poop (breaking down organic things)
Worms found in the soil around the Zambezi River
